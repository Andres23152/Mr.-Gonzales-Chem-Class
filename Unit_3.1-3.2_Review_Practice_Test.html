Mole Concept Outline ▼

1. Basic Definitions

  • Mole (n): 1 mole = (6.022 times 10^23) particles.
  • Molar Mass (M): Mass in grams of 1 mole of a substance (g/mol).

2. Key Formulas

  • n = mass/molar mass
  • mass = n * molar mass
  • # of particles = n * (6.022 x 10^23)

Use these relationships to move between mass in grams, moles, and number of particles.

Practice Test ▼

Answer the following 25 questions to test your understanding of the mole concept.

  1. Calculate the molar mass of LiCl. Show Answer

    Li: ~6.94 g/mol   Cl: ~35.45 g/mol
    M(LiCl) = 6.94 + 35.45 = 42.39 g/mol

  2. Calculate the molar mass of NH3. Show Answer

    N: ~14.01 g/mol   H: ~1.01 g/mol
    M(NH3) = 14.01 + 3(1.01) = 14.01 + 3.03 = 17.04 g/mol

  3. Calculate the molar mass of Ca(NO3)2. Show Answer

    Ca: 40.08 g/mol   N: 14.01 g/mol   O: 16.00 g/mol
    M(Ca(NO3)2) = 40.08 + 2(14.01 + 3×16.00)
    = 40.08 + 2(14.01 + 48.00)
    = 40.08 + 2(62.01)
    = 40.08 + 124.02
    = 164.10 g/mol

  4. Calculate the molar mass of C6H12O6. Show Answer

    C: 12.01 g/mol, H: 1.01 g/mol, O: 16.00 g/mol
    M(C6H12O6) = 6(12.01) + 12(1.01) + 6(16.00)
    = 72.06 + 12.12 + 96.00
    = 180.18 g/mol (approx.)

  5. Calculate the molar mass of Al2(SO4)3. Show Answer

    Al: 26.98 g/mol, S: 32.06 g/mol, O: 16.00 g/mol
    M(Al2(SO4)3) = 2(26.98) + 3[32.06 + (4×16.00)]
    = 53.96 + 3(32.06 + 64.00)
    = 53.96 + 3(96.06)
    = 53.96 + 288.18
    = 342.14 g/mol

  6. How many moles are in 25.0 g of NaCl? Show Answer

    M(NaCl) ≈ 58.44 g/mol
    n = mass / M = 25.0 g / 58.44 g/mol ≈ 0.428 mol

  7. How many moles are in 50.0 g of CO2? Show Answer

    M(CO2) ≈ 44.01 g/mol
    n = 50.0 g / 44.01 g/mol ≈ 1.136 mol

  8. How many moles are in 16.0 g of O2? Show Answer

    M(O2) = 2 × 16.00 = 32.00 g/mol
    n = 16.0 g / 32.00 g/mol = 0.50 mol

  9. A sample contains 145 g of KBr. How many moles of KBr are present? Show Answer

    M(KBr) ≈ 119.0 g/mol (K ~39.10, Br ~79.90)
    n = 145 g / 119.0 g/mol ≈ 1.22 mol

  10. A laboratory has 9.8 g of H2SO4. How many moles of sulfuric acid are in the sample? Show Answer

    M(H2SO4) ≈ 98.09 g/mol
    n = 9.8 g / 98.09 g/mol ≈ 0.10 mol

  11. How many grams are in 0.75 mol of MgCl2? Show Answer

    M(MgCl2) ≈ 95.21 g/mol (Mg ~24.31, Cl ~35.45 × 2)
    mass = 0.75 mol × 95.21 g/mol ≈ 71.41 g

  12. If you have 2.00 mol of Fe2O3, what is the mass in grams? Show Answer

    M(Fe2O3) = 2(55.85) + 3(16.00) = 111.70 + 48.00 = 159.70 g/mol
    mass = 2.00 mol × 159.70 g/mol = 319.4 g

  13. You have 0.300 mol of glucose (C6H12O6). What is the mass in grams? Show Answer

    M(C6H12O6) ≈ 180.18 g/mol
    mass = 0.300 mol × 180.18 g/mol ≈ 54.05 g

  14. A container holds 2.5 moles of CaCO3. Calculate the mass in grams. Show Answer

    M(CaCO3) = 40.08 + 12.01 + 3(16.00) = 40.08 + 12.01 + 48.00 = 100.09 g/mol
    mass = 2.5 mol × 100.09 g/mol = 250.225 g ≈ 250.2 g

  15. How many grams are in 0.0500 mol of CuSO4? Show Answer

    M(CuSO4) = 63.55 + 32.06 + 4(16.00) = 63.55 + 32.06 + 64.00 = 159.61 g/mol
    mass = 0.0500 mol × 159.61 g/mol = 7.98 g

  16. How many molecules are in 3.0 moles of CO? Show Answer

    # molecules = (3.0 mol) × (6.022 × 1023)
    ≈ 1.81 × 1024 molecules

  17. How many formula units are in 0.25 mol of Na2SO4? Show Answer

    # formula units = 0.25 mol × 6.022 × 1023
    = 1.51 × 1023 formula units

  18. You have 2.4 moles of He. How many atoms of helium are there? Show Answer

    # atoms = 2.4 mol × 6.022 × 1023
    ≈ 1.45 × 1024 atoms He

  19. How many atoms of carbon are there in 0.40 mol of C? Show Answer

    # atoms = 0.40 mol × 6.022 × 1023
    ≈ 2.41 × 1023 atoms C

  20. A sample contains 0.10 mol of H2O. How many molecules is this? Show Answer

    # molecules = 0.10 mol × 6.022 × 1023
    = 6.022 × 1022 molecules H2O

  21. A sample of Al foil weighs 5.40 g.
    (a) Calculate the number of moles of Al in the sample.
    (b) How many atoms of Al would that be? Show Answer

    (a) M(Al) ≈ 26.98 g/mol
    n = 5.40 g / 26.98 g/mol ≈ 0.200 mol

    (b) Atoms = 0.200 mol × 6.022 × 1023 ≈ 1.20 × 1023 atoms

  22. A container holds 2.00 × 1024 molecules of N2.
    (a) How many moles of N2 is this?
    (b) What is the mass of this sample of N2? Show Answer

    (a) n = (2.00 × 1024) / (6.022 × 1023) ≈ 3.32 mol
    (b) M(N2) = 28.02 g/mol
    mass = 3.32 mol × 28.02 g/mol ≈ 92.9 g

  23. A sealed tank contains 1.5 moles of Ne gas at 25°C and 2.0 atm. A second sealed tank contains 1.5 moles of Kr gas at 25°C and 2.0 atm. Which tank has the greater density and why? Show Answer

    Both tanks have the same moles, T, and P, thus the same V (from ideal gas law). However, Kr has a higher molar mass than Ne, so the same number of moles of Kr will have a greater mass in the same volume. Therefore, the Kr tank has the greater density.

  24. You have 13 g of N2, 27 g of Al, and 16 g of CH4. Rank these from least to greatest in terms of (a) moles and (b) total atoms. Show Answer

    (a) Moles:
    - N2 has M ≈ 28 g/mol ⇒ 13 g / 28 g/mol ≈ 0.46 mol
    - Al has M ≈ 26.98 g/mol ⇒ 27 g / 26.98 g/mol ≈ 1.00 mol
    - CH4 has M ≈ 16.04 g/mol ⇒ 16 g / 16.04 g/mol ≈ 1.0 mol
    Order (least→greatest): N2 (0.46 mol) < Al (1.0 mol) ≈ CH4 (1.0 mol)

    (b) Total Atoms:
    For each substance:
    - N2: 0.46 mol × (6.022 × 1023 molecules/mol) × (2 atoms/molecule)
    ≈ 0.46 × 2.77 × 1024 = 1.27 × 1024 atoms
    - Al: 1.0 mol × (6.022 × 1023 atoms/mol)
    = 6.022 × 1023 atoms
    - CH4: 1.0 mol × (6.022 × 1023 molecules/mol) × (5 atoms/molecule)
    = 3.01 × 1024 atoms
    So, total atoms (least→greatest): Al < N2 < CH4.

  25. A cell contains 5.00 × 1015 molecules of a fatty acid (molar mass = 750 g/mol). (a) How many moles is this? (b) What is the mass in grams? Show Answer

    (a) n = (5.00 × 1015) / (6.022 × 1023) ≈ 8.30 × 10-9 mol
    (b) mass = n × 750 g/mol ≈ 8.30 × 10-9 mol × 750 g/mol
    ≈ 6.23 × 10-6 g
